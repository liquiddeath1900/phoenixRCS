Types of Storm Damage We Restore in Maryland

Maryland experiences various severe weather events throughout the year. Our team is prepared for all types of storm damage:

⛈️ Severe Thunderstorms

High winds, hail, and heavy rain can cause roof damage, window breakage, siding damage, and flooding. These are the most common storm events in our area.

🌪️ Tornado Damage

While less common in Maryland, tornadoes can cause catastrophic damage requiring complete reconstruction. We handle everything from minor repairs to total rebuilds.

🌀 Hurricane & Tropical Storms

Maryland's Atlantic coast location means occasional hurricane impacts. High winds, storm surge, and flooding require specialized restoration approaches.

🧊 Hail Damage

Hail can damage roofs, siding, windows, and HVAC equipment. Our team identifies all hail damage and ensures complete repair through insurance claims.

❄️ Ice Storms

Ice accumulation can damage roofs, gutters, and trees. Falling limbs and ice dams create unique restoration challenges requiring specialized techniques.

🌧️ Flash Flooding

Heavy rainfall can overwhelm drainage systems, causing sudden flooding. Rapid water extraction and drying are critical to prevent further structural damage.

Storm Preparation Tips for Maryland Property Owners

While we're here when storms strike, preparation can minimize damage and speed recovery:

🏠 Pre-Storm Property Preparation

  • Regular roof inspections to identify and repair weak points
  • Tree trimming to remove branches threatening structures
  • Gutter cleaning to prevent water backup and overflow
  • Window protection plans including shutters or plywood
  • Secure outdoor furniture and equipment that can become projectiles
  • Generator preparation for extended power outages

📋 Documentation for Insurance

  • Maintain current photos/video of your property exterior
  • Keep receipts for recent roof, siding, or window upgrades
  • Document valuable landscaping and outdoor equipment
  • Maintain updated property insurance coverage
  • Know your insurance deductible and coverage limits

⚡ After Storm Safety

  • Stay away from downed power lines - assume they're energized
  • Don't walk on damaged roofs or enter structurally damaged buildings
  • Document damage with photos before cleanup begins
  • Contact professionals before attempting major cleanup
  • Watch for hidden damage like foundation shifts or structural issues
