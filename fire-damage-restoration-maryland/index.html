Types of Fire and Smoke Damage We Restore

Different types of fires create different restoration challenges. Our team is trained to handle all varieties:

üç≥ Kitchen Fires (Protein Fires)

Common in Prince George's and Montgomery County homes. Protein fires from cooking create sticky, pungent residues that require specialized cleaning techniques. The odor is particularly difficult to eliminate and can affect the entire home.

ü™µ Wood/Paper Fires (Dry Smoke)

Fast-burning fires that create dry, powdery residue. While easier to clean than wet smoke, dry smoke can spread throughout a structure via HVAC systems and requires thorough cleaning of all surfaces.

üõãÔ∏è Synthetic Material Fires (Wet Smoke)

Smoldering fires from plastics, fabrics, and synthetic materials create thick, sticky residues. This type of smoke damage is the most challenging to clean and often requires extensive restoration efforts.

‚õΩ Fuel Oil/Chemical Fires

Petroleum-based fires create unique restoration challenges. The residues are often toxic and require specialized handling and disposal procedures. These fires often require more extensive air quality testing.

üåø Puffback (Furnace Fires)

Common in older homes throughout our Maryland service area. Furnace malfunctions can distribute soot throughout a home via ductwork, requiring extensive HVAC cleaning and restoration.

Fire Prevention Tips for Maryland Homeowners

While we're here when you need fire restoration, prevention is always better. Here are key fire safety tips for Maryland homes:

üè† Home Fire Safety Checklist

  • ‚úÖ Install smoke detectors on every level and in every bedroom
  • ‚úÖ Test smoke detector batteries monthly
  • ‚úÖ Keep fire extinguishers in kitchen, garage, and basement
  • ‚úÖ Clean dryer vents regularly (major fire source)
  • ‚úÖ Have furnaces and chimneys inspected annually
  • ‚úÖ Maintain electrical systems - replace old wiring
  • ‚úÖ Store flammable materials safely
  • ‚úÖ Create and practice fire escape plans

üîå Electrical Fire Prevention

Many Maryland homes, especially older ones in Prince George's County, have aging electrical systems. Warning signs include frequently tripping breakers, flickering lights, burning odors, or warm outlets. Have electrical systems inspected by licensed electricians.

üç≥ Kitchen Fire Safety

Kitchen fires are the most common residential fires. Never leave cooking unattended, keep combustible materials away from stovetops, and ensure proper ventilation. Install range hood fire suppression systems in high-use kitchens.
